Sustainable farming practices can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
Crop rotation helps maintain soil health and reduces the need for synthetic fertilizers.
Agroforestry combines trees and shrubs with crops or livestock to create more sustainable land-use systems.
Conservation tillage reduces soil erosion and improves water retention in the soil.
Integrated pest management reduces the need for harmful pesticides.
Cover crops help prevent soil erosion and improve soil fertility.
Precision agriculture uses technology to make farming more efficient and reduce waste.
Sustainable irrigation practices can significantly reduce water usage in agriculture.
Organic farming practices promote biodiversity and soil health.
Permaculture design principles can create sustainable and self-sufficient agricultural ecosystems.
